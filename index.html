Look on the upper banner, that is a male Habropoda sutepensis Cockerell, 1929, a rare bee that have been found only once in the world from Doi Suthep, Chiang Mai

Habropoda sutepensis Cockerell, 1929 is a rare bees designated under tribe Anthophoriini, similar to the famous blue-banded bees (genus Amegilla). This bees are rare, endemic to the sub-tropical to sub-temperate area of East asia, particularly ranging from the eastern India to Taiwan (but except Japan). Its favors highland environment especially on higher altitudes and often detected from the mountain ranges. Throughout the history, The series of comprehensive works are from Lieftinck's and Wu's publications. Considering for its rareness in the nature, it is also evidently often co-occurrence with cuckoo bee species that (cleptoparasitic bees especially Tetralonioidella). Thus, their presences emphasize how complex the diversity of respective environment.
